What these results mean to me

Some of these results reflect my behavior in some very minor aspects of my day-to-day life. For example, the Myers-Briggs test correctly predicted how I prefer to learn how to spell difficult words. When I was younger, I learnt how to spell beautiful through song, “Mr.B Mr.E Mr.AUT” iful, in a certain rhythm. The accuracy was pretty surprising. The learning style test has me put into action a new study plan to try and improve my study habits as I’d like to see myself get through content more efficiently – Shorter yet more frequent breaks. As for the creativity test I took, I struggle working with large amounts of information in one go which contradicts the result I got. I get overwhelmed relatively easily and wouldn’t consider that result entirely accurate, however, I would argue that the persistence aspect I scored high on is relatively accurate. I tend to have this urge to complete a task I’ve begun, irrelevant of time spent. For example, I was once working on coding a Vigenère Cipher, however, I was going about it logically and not through the mathematical equation which can be found on Wikipedia. I spent a solid 2 or 3 days trying to get it to work and having almost given up, I then read the Wikipedia page on it and discovered the equation which allowed me to solve the problem in about an hour at best.

How my results influence my behaviour in a team

I would argue that the results reflect a fair portion of qualities that would be preferrable for teamwork. For instance, on the Mind section of the Myers-Briggs test I was considered an extravert. This means I enjoy working alongside other people and I’d argue it personally motivates my work ethic when people around me are also putting in 110%. My only concern is that according to the Myers-Briggs test, I prefer a structured plan with a detailed timeline. When working in a team, I may struggle to work alongside others if tasks are scattered and we lack a structured plan. In contrast, having the observant trait on the Myers-Briggs test means I’m more focused on what we would be working on as a team in the moment. I see myself as having relatively good attention to detail which I would consider an asset when working in a group. According to the learning style test, I have this aspect of flexibility due to being equally both a visual and a tactile learner. Furthermore, the creativity test claims I have the ‘persistence’ trait which resembles a strong work ethic. This could come in useful when working in a group as working around others can cause me to indirectly motivate others around me and vice versa as well.

How I would take my results into account

When forming a team, I’d like others in my group to either balance or compliment my traits. So, my ability to be decisive, thorough, and attentive to details may come in useful at times, however, I may appreciate someone else in the group supporting my inability to look at the bigger picture of the task at hand. Moreover, persistence may be good, but I can see it causing some form of tunnel vision at times. Having frequent meetings discussing progress would be very useful to take a step back and analyze a situation to check whether or not progress has been efficient or if things need to be changed up. All in all, I’d like to see a fair balance of personality traits as I believe a diverse team would work best and complement each other’s talents.
